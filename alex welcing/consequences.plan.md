## The Work Ahead

### Phase 0: Structural Mapping (1–2 sessions)
**Goal**: Know what we're building before we build it.

**Deliverables**:
1. **`consequences.chapter_map.md`** — For each of the 15 Reaching chapters:
   - Does it survive? (Yes / No / Merge / Split)
   - If yes: what beats stay, what beats go, what new beats are invented
   - If no: where does its thematic weight get redistributed?
   - Word count target (2,000–3,000 words per chapter)
2. **`consequences.new_beats.md`** — Invented material for the YA structure:
   - **Friendship arc**: Meet-cute → tension → near-break → test under pressure → ?
   - **Action set pieces**: 2–3 per act, comic-book kinetic, 70s pulp swashbuckling
   - **School / authority dynamics**: Guidance counselor, teacher, parent, security drones
   - **Satirical set pieces**: The machine's grading algorithm, the diagnostic competition, the college essay AI, the "competence evaluation"
3. **The action inventory**: Specific set pieces that need 70s pulp energy:
   - Chases (drone pursuit, hallway escapes)
   - Confrontations (verbal fights with authority, machine challenges)
   - Physical challenges (competitions, escapes, infiltrations)
   - The "tantalizing" principle: each action scene teases the next escalation

**Why this phase first**: Rewriting Chapter 1 without knowing what Chapter 5 needs is how you write 15 disconnected stories instead of one novel.

---

### Phase 1: The Foundation (Chapters 1–3)
**Goal**: Establish the voice, the world, the friendship, and the action engine in stone.

**Approach**:
- **Chapter 1**: Rewrite from scratch. The 2.1mm opacity → a high school diagnostic competition or class assignment. The same emotional beat (the machine found what she missed) but aged down, sped up, and given a satirical edge. **First action hook**: Adaeze and Maya in a physical or competitive confrontation with the machine.
- **Chapter 2**: Introduce the friendship dynamic. Establish Maya's role (the one who sneaks in windows, the one who smells like vanilla, the one who doesn't ask before drinking coffee). First action set piece — a chase or a challenge that tests them together.
- **Chapter 3**: First major turning point. The machine's superiority becomes undeniable. The friendship is tested — Maya tries to comfort; Adaeze can't be comforted. An action beat shows the gap between them widening.

**Deliverable**: `consequences.ch01.md`, `consequences.ch02.md`, `consequences.ch03.md` — each 2,000–3,000 words.

**Quality gate**: After Chapter 3, we stop and audit.
- Does the voice hold? (Hemingway contrast principle, not mechanical short sentences)
- Does Adaeze feel 17? (Gauche literary references, deadpan humor, unsentimental)
- Does the friendship feel earned? (Specific details: taco socks, asthma, window-sneaking, the coffee she never asks for)
- Does the action feel kinetic? (70s pulp swashbuckling: propulsive, tantalizing, strong verbs, panel-like paragraphs)
- If the answer is no, we fix the blueprint before writing Chapter 4.

---

### Phase 2: The Middle (Chapters 4–8)
**Goal**: Build the satirical engine, deepen the friendship, escalate the action.

**Approaches**:
- **Chapters 4–5**: Satirical set pieces with action energy. The guidance counselor confrontation as a verbal fight scene. The machine's grading algorithm as a competitive challenge. The "competence evaluation" as a bureaucratic action sequence.
- **Chapters 6–7**: Friendship complication. Maya sees something Adaeze doesn't. The machine threatens something personal. An action set piece where they have to work together but disagree on how.
- **Chapter 8**: Midpoint reversal. The thing Adaeze thought she was fighting for turns out to be the wrong thing. The satire turns inward. An action beat that physically manifests the reversal.

**Deliverable**: `consequences.ch04.md` through `consequences.ch08.md`.

**Quality gate**: After Chapter 8, audit the friendship arc. Is it tested by pressure? Is the edge-of-gratuity content earned? Does the action escalate with 70s pulp energy?

---

### Phase 3: The End (Chapters 9–15)
**Goal**: The machine wins. The human adapts. The friendship survives or is transformed. The ending is unsentimental.

**Approaches**:
- **Chapters 9–10**: Action escalation. The comic-book fight scene — maybe a literal chase, a confrontation with the machine's physical form, a competitive climax. 70s pulp swashbuckling: kinetic, breathless, tantalizing.
- **Chapters 11–12**: Tragic beat. The loss is physicalized. The friendship is the thing that gets damaged. The aftermath is brief. The silence is the point.
- **Chapters 13–14**: The aftermath. No easy resolution. Adaeze doesn't "learn a lesson." She survives, possibly, and the survival is questionable. The friendship is changed, not healed.
- **Chapter 15**: Refused resolution. No "the machine had a soul." No "she learned to be herself." Something ambiguous. Something earned. The final image: a hand reaching toward a screen. The hand stops. The screen stays on. The hand doesn't withdraw. The hand doesn't touch.

**Deliverable**: `consequences.ch09.md` through `consequences.ch15.md`.

**Quality gate**: The unsentimental test. Does the ending earn its ambiguity through loss? Is the friendship transformed by the action, not just preserved?

---

### Phase 4: Assembly & Polish
**Goal**: One manuscript, one voice, consistent.

**Work**:
1. Read all chapters in sequence. Mark voice drift, plot holes, friendship inconsistencies.
2. Pass for the friendship arc — does it resolve honestly? (YA friendship doesn't need a perfect reunion; it needs honesty.)
3. Pass for the satirical targets — are we still punching up? Did any joke become mean?
4. Pass for the action rhythm — is the kinetic energy consistent? Do the 70s pulp set pieces feel propulsive, not forced?
5. Pass for the Hemingway sound-off — can the emotional beats be understood with lyrical passages removed?
6. Pass for word count — total under 45,000? Per chapter 2,000–3,000?

**Deliverable**: `consequences_manuscript.md` — complete.
