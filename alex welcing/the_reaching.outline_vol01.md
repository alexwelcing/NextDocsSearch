## Working Window

### ← Ch 1 [DONE] (2,559w)
**Summary**: Adaeze Nwosu, a radiologist with 23 years of experience, misses a 2.1mm pulmonary nodule that an AI overlay flags instantly. Her certainty becomes the obstacle. She begins reading with the overlay always on, becoming a judge of disagreements rather than a primary reader. Her hand makes phantom scrolling motions in the dark.
**Hook-out**: Her hand is a memory of expertise, the last resident of an evacuated country — the reaching has already begun, though she does not know it yet.

### ← Ch 2 [DONE] (2,179w)
**Summary**: Maren Solberg, a literary translator, receives a machine translation so good she changes only 8% of the text. She discovers her own published translations are in the training corpus — her excellence fueled her obsolescence. She visits her mother in Ulvik and realizes the machine can translate but cannot gather. She opens the Translation Garden in Oslo — a space for deliberate, unpaid, communal translation.
**Hook-out**: The garden is where people come to fail at something together, and the failure is the value — the reaching has begun.

### ← Ch 3 [DONE] (2,395w)
**Summary**: Tomáš Horák, a blacksmith for 31 years, installs an AI forge assistant that corrects his tremor — and in doing so, erases the signature that makes his work his. He reconfigures the Anvil to map his imperfections rather than correct them. A fifteen-year-old girl named Zuzana arrives in the rain and refuses to leave. He takes her on as an apprentice.
**Hook-out**: Zuzana strikes her first piece of steel — the sound is not yet a song, but the promise of one. The reaching is the point.
