> **Date**: 2026-06-17  
> **Source**: The Reaching (15 chapters, 4 POVs, ~70K words)  
> **Target**: Consequences (15 chapters, 1 POV, ~40K words)  
> **Genre**: YA Science Fiction / Satirical / Action  
> **Core**: Friendship between Adaeze (16–17) and Maya (16–17)  
> **Action**: Comic-book kinetic, 70s pulp swashbuckling  
> **Tone**: Satirical future, unsentimental, propulsive

---

## The Big Idea

Adaeze is the best young diagnostic reader in the country. She enters a competition to prove it. The machine wins. She spends the next 15 chapters trying to beat the machine, failing, and discovering that the only thing she has that the machine doesn't is Maya — her best friend, her accomplice, her witness. The friendship is tested by pressure, not preserved by it. The ending is ambiguous: she survives, possibly, but survival is questionable.

---

## Character Arcs

### Adaeze Nwosu
**What she wants**: To be the best at something. To be seen as competent. To matter.
**What she loses**: The belief that competence = identity. The certainty that effort is enough.
**What she becomes**: Someone who reaches without knowing if there's anything to grasp. Someone who keeps going because stopping would mean admitting it's over.

**The arc in three acts**:
- **Act 1**: She is the best. She loses to the machine. She tries to fight back.
- **Act 2**: She escalates. She breaks rules. She risks the friendship. She loses anyway.
- **Act 3**: She stops fighting the machine and starts fighting the system that made the machine inevitable. The fight is smaller. The stakes are higher. The ending is refused.

### Maya Chen
**What she wants**: To be a good friend. To be useful. To not be left behind.
**What she loses**: The belief that support is enough. The innocence of not understanding.
**What she becomes**: The one thing Adaeze has that the machine doesn't. But she's not a fix. She's a witness.

**The arc in three acts**:
- **Act 1**: She supports without fully understanding. She thinks Adaeze is being dramatic. She is wrong but not cruel.
- **Act 2**: She tries to help in the wrong ways. She makes mistakes. She accidentally helps the machine. She and Adaeze fight.
- **Act 3**: She stops trying to fix Adaeze and starts just being there. The being there is not enough. It is everything.

## Action Set Pieces (The 70s Pulp Swashbuckling)

### Act 1
1. **The competition (Ch 1)**: The Machine Games. Timer. Crowd. The walk of shame. The bus ride home.
2. **The break-in (Ch 3)**: Adaeze and Maya sneak into the school's diagnostic wing after hours. They try to access the machine's training data. Security drones wake up. The chase begins.

### Act 2
3. **The raid and chase (Ch 7)**: The human-only reading club is raided by authorities. Adaeze and Maya run through the school, through vents, past the cafeteria, into the parking lot.
4. **The rematch (Ch 9)**: Live broadcast. Adaeze and Maya as a team vs. SENTINEL-7. They lose. The loss is public, humiliating, and final.

### Act 3
5. **The final competition (Ch 13)**: The nationals. Adaeze is expected to lose. She enters. She reads. She finds the anomaly. But she refuses to press submit. She sits there. The timer runs out. She is disqualified. The disqualification is the act of humanity.

## Key Rules for Writing

1. **One chapter at a time.** Read the source chapter, identify the emotional beat, write the new chapter.
2. **The friendship is the through-line.** Every chapter must test, strain, or transform the friendship.
3. **Action is character.** Adaeze doesn't just feel things — she runs, she breaks in, she refuses to submit.
4. **Satire punches up.** The machine is not the villain. The system that made the machine inevitable is the villain. The machine is just better.
5. **The ending is refused.** No "she learned to be herself." No "the machine had a soul." Ambiguity earned through loss.
