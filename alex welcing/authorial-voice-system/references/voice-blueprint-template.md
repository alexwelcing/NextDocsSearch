# Voice Blueprint Template

Use this as the structural scaffold for every voice blueprint. Fill each section with project-specific mechanics. Replace bracketed placeholders with concrete rules. Vague adjectives are not acceptable — each entry must be a measurable or actionable constraint.

---

## 1. Persona Profile

### 1.1 Identity
- **Name / pseudonym**: [Who is writing? A persona, a narrator, or the implied author?]
- **Age & era**: [What generation formed this voice? What cultural references are native?]
- **Day job**: [What does this voice do when not writing? How does that job shape attention?]
- **Emotional temperature**: [Warm, cold, ironic, earnest, detached, obsessive?]

### 1.2 Beliefs & Biases
- **What this voice believes about people**: [Cynical? Hopeful? believes everyone is hiding something?]
- **What this voice believes about love**: [Redemptive? destructive? comic? transactional?]
- **What this voice believes about competence**: [Worships it? mourns it? finds it ridiculous?]
- **What this voice cannot forgive**: [The unforgivable act — defines the moral floor]

### 1.3 Loves & Obsessions
- **Objects of attention**: [Hands, light, machinery, weather, food, silence — what does this voice notice?]
- **Sensory hierarchy**: [Which sense dominates? sight, touch, sound, smell, taste?]
- **Recurring motifs**: [3–5 images that will recur across the narrative]

### 1.4 Voice Signature (50-word exemplar)
Write a single paragraph that sounds like this voice. This is the north star. Every test passage must rhyme with this paragraph.

## 2. Diction Register

### 2.1 Allowed Words
- **Core vocabulary**: [20–30 words this voice uses frequently and naturally]
- **Domain-specific diction**: [Medical, legal, culinary, mechanical — what technical lexicon is native?]
- **Loanwords & foreign terms**: [Any non-English words, and how they are deployed]

### 2.2 Forbidden Words
- **AI vocabulary**: [e.g., "delve," "tapestry," "landscape," "multifaceted," "testament" — list explicitly]
- **Promotional language**: ["groundbreaking," "compelling," "poignant" — any word that praises itself]
- **Inflated abstractions**: ["the human condition," "the weight of existence" — ban vague grandeur]
- **Overused metaphors**: ["rollercoaster of emotions," "journey," "battle" — any dead metaphor]

### 2.3 Register Shifts
- **When the voice rises**: [Stress, revelation, anger — what triggers formal elevation?]
- **When the voice falls**: [Intimacy, exhaustion, defeat — what triggers plainness?]
- **Code-switching rules**: [When can this voice borrow from another register? How is the transition marked?]

### 2.4 Metaphor Preferences
- **Source domains**: [What fields does this voice draw metaphors from? Medicine? Cooking? Mechanics?]
- **Metaphor density**: [1 per 150 words? 1 per 50? None at all?]
- **Forbidden comparisons**: [What must this voice never compare to?]

## 3. Sentence Architecture

### 3.1 Length Distribution
- **Short**: [% of sentences, word count range, typical use case]
- **Medium**: [% of sentences, word count range, typical use case]
- **Long**: [% of sentences, word count range, typical use case]
- **Rule**: [e.g., "Never two long sentences in a row. Always follow a long with a short."]

### 3.2 Rhythm Patterns
- **Default cadence**: [e.g., "Staccato — short declarative bursts, then a longer breath."]
- **Stress patterns**: [Does this voice favor iambs, trochees, or free rhythm?]
- **Sentence openers**: [Common first-word patterns — "The," "She," "When," "It was" — and frequency caps]
- **Sentence closers**: [How do sentences end? With a noun? a verb? an image?]

### 3.3 Punctuation Voice
- **Periods**: [Steady, frequent, rare?]
- **Commas**: [Sparse, generous, breath-marking?]
- **Em-dashes**: [Max per 1000 chars — e.g., ≤ 3. When used? interruption, apposition, breath?]
- **Semicolons**: [Allowed? Forbidden? Only for specific structural purposes?]
- **Fragments**: [Allowed? In what contexts?]
- **Colon usage**: [Rare? Frequent? Only for lists or also for revelation?]

### 3.4 Clause Structure
- **Subordination ratio**: [How many dependent clauses per sentence? Max?]
- **Coordination preference**: [Polysyndeton (and, and, and) vs. asyndeton (no conjunctions)?]
- **Embedding depth**: [Max levels of nesting — e.g., "The man who knew the woman who saw the thing" → forbidden?]

## 4. Humor Mechanics

### 4.1 Irony Mode
- **Dramatic irony**: [Audience knows more than character? How is it set up?]
- **Situational irony**: [Expectation vs. outcome — how is the gap calibrated?]
- **Verbal irony**: [Sarcasm? understatement? overstatement? deadpan?]
- **Ironic distance**: [How close is the narrator to the material? Close enough to care, far enough to see the absurdity?]

### 4.2 Juxtaposition Rules
- **High-low collisions**: [What registers collide for comic effect? e.g., scientific + vulgar, bureaucratic + intimate]
- **Scale shifts**: [Sudden zoom from cosmic to domestic, or vice versa?]
- **Anachronism**: [Allowed? In what dosage?]

### 4.3 Comic Timing
- **Setup-to-punchline distance**: [Immediate? delayed? distributed across a scene?]
- **Sentence-level comedy**: [Comic word order, unexpected final words, rhythmic mismatch]
- **Scene-level comedy**: [When does a scene become comic? What is the turning point?]
- **Repetition & variation**: [Running gags? Callbacks? How many repetitions before payoff?]

### 4.4 Satirical Targets (if applicable)
- **Target 1**: [What institution, behavior, or attitude is being skewered?]
- **Target 2**: [etc.]
- **Satirical tone**: [Gentle, savage, surgical, gleeful?]
- **Escape hatch**: [Does the satire allow the target humanity, or is it pure indictment?]

## 5. Tragedy Mechanics

### 5.1 Loss Architecture
- **What is lost**: [Competence, love, innocence, language, identity — what is the stakes?]
- **How it is lost**: [Suddenly, gradually, by betrayal, by erosion, by choice?]
- **Who witnesses it**: [The loser? a bystander? the reader alone?]

### 5.2 Emotional Beats
- **The beat before the blow**: [What does the character feel right before tragedy lands?]
- **The blow itself**: [How is it rendered? Directly? obliquely? through a physical detail?]
- **The aftermath**: [How long does the voice dwell? What is the ratio of grief to silence?]

### 5.3 Restraint Rules
- **What is not said**: [The tragedy this voice cannot name directly — how is it approached sideways?]
- **Physicalization**: [How is grief made visible? hands, posture, breath, objects?]
- **The unweeping rule**: [Is crying allowed on page? Under what conditions?]

### 5.4 Payoff Design
- **Catharsis path**: [How does the reader get release? Through character recognition? Through reader inference?]
- **Hope residue**: [After tragedy, what remains? Nothing? A habit? A gesture?]
- **The final image**: [What image closes the tragic arc?]

## 6. Visual Sensibility

### 6.1 Camera Distance
- **Default lens**: [Wide, medium, close, macro?]
- **Zoom rules**: [When does the voice pull back? When does it push in?]
- **POV stability**: [Fixed? drifting? unreliable?]

### 6.2 Light & Color
- **Light quality**: [Warm, cold, flat, chiaroscuro, practical?]
- **Color vocabulary**: [What colors does this voice name? What does it avoid?]
- **Light as emotion**: [How does light signal interior state?]

### 6.3 Texture & Motion
- **Surface attention**: [What textures recur? smooth, rough, wet, dry?]
- **Motion description**: [How are bodies and objects described in motion?]
- **Stillness**: [How is the absence of motion rendered?]

### 6.4 The Sound-Off Test
- **Can the story be understood with dialogue removed?** [What visual information carries meaning independent of speech?]
- **Visual motifs**: [Recurring images that carry thematic weight without words?]

## Audit Scoring Rubric

For each test passage, score 1–5 on:
- Persona consistency
- Diction adherence
- Sentence architecture compliance
- Humor mechanics (if applicable)
- Tragedy mechanics (if applicable)
- Visual sensibility
- Anti-pattern absence

**Pass threshold**: ≥ 4 on all dimensions.
**Fail action**: Revise the blueprint rule or regenerate the passage.
